Research Focus

Bridging behavioural
science and health practice

Chartered Psychologist specialising in cognitive psychology, gerontology, and health-related applied social science. My work spans micro-level behavioural intervention research — RCTs, feasibility trials, MRTs — through to macro-level population health using large longitudinal cohorts and advanced quantitative methods.

01
Ageing, Dementia & Caregiving
Stress, wellbeing, dyadic processes, behavioural and psychological symptoms of dementia, and evidence-based caregiver support programs.
02
Intervention Development & Evaluation
Randomized controlled trials, feasibility and pilot studies, micro-randomized trials (MRTs), JITAIs, and hybrid effectiveness–implementation designs.
03
Technology-Enabled Mental Health
Immersive virtual reality, VR-based mindfulness, digital mindfulness interventions, wearables, and digital therapeutics.
04
Nature-Based & Lifestyle Interventions
Forest bathing and nature exposure, horticultural therapy, restorative environments, and lifestyle-based mental health promotion.
05
Population Health & Social Determinants
Longitudinal cohort research (CHARLS, ELSA), life-course influences, social participation, depression, cognition, and functional health trajectories.
06
Quantitative Methods & Measurement
Longitudinal and multilevel modelling, dyadic analysis, causal inference, psychometrics, and physiological biomarkers such as HRV.
